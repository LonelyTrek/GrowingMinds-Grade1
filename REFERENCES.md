### *The 4-Hour Chef* by Tim Ferriss
> **Core Concept**: DiSSS (Deconstruction, Selection, Sequencing, Stakes)
> **Application**:
> *   **Deconstruction**: Breaking down "Math" into atomic skills (Number Sense, Spatial, Logic).
> *   **Selection**: Focusing on the "Minimum Effective Dose" (e.g., skipping rote calculation if the concept is mastered).
> *   **Sequencing**: Why we teach "visualizing 10" before "writing 10".

### *Mindset: The New Psychology of Success* by Carol S. Dweck
> **Core Concept**: Growth Mindset vs. Fixed Mindset
> **Application**:
> *   **Praise Effort, Not Intelligence**: In our `daily-log.md`, we record *how* they solved it, not just if they got it right.
> *   **The Power of "Yet"**: "You don't understand subtraction *yet*."

### *Thinking, Fast and Slow* by Daniel Kahneman
> **Core Concept**: System 1 (Fast/Intuitive) vs. System 2 (Slow/Analytical)
> **Application**:
> *   **Fluency Trap**: When a child answers instantly (System 1), it might mean the task is too easy (`#Fluency`). We need to push them into System 2 (`#ZoneOfProximal`) where real learning happens.
> *   **Cognitive Strain**: Designing puzzles that force them to stop and think.

### *Make It Stick: The Science of Successful Learning* by Peter C. Brown et al.
> **Core Concept**: Active Retrieval, Spaced Repetition, Interleaving
> **Application**:
> *   **Interleaving**: We mix Logic, Geometry, and Arithmetic in one week, rather than doing "Math Month".
> *   **Retrieval**: The "I Am the Teacher" activity on Fridays forces the child to retrieve and explain knowledge, which is stronger than re-reading.
